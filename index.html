Introduction

The finite element method is a powerful tool for analyzing wide range of engineering problems. It has got wide variety of applications ranging from stress and deformation analysis of buildings and aircraft structures, predicting cracks/fractures, thermal problems etc. In this method, a complex region is discretized into small geometrical shapes. The governing relationships and material properties are considered over these elements. These finite elements are then assembled and solved to give us the approximate behaviour of the continuum.

There are two main motivations for learning the finite element method. One is to write a FEM solver which few people would attempt. The second is to learn the method to understand how a commercial FEM solver analyzes a particular problem. This course is designed for this purpose. Before we dive into FEM, we will have a look on the basics of solid mechanics.
